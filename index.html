Synopsis

1841. Solomon Northup (Chiwetel Ejiofor) is a freed slave who lives in peace with his wife and children. One day, after accepting a job that takes him to another city, he is kidnapped and chained. Sold as a slave, Solomon must overcome physical and emotional humiliation to survive. Over the course of twelve years he has passed through two masters, Ford (Benedict Cumberbatch) and Edwin Epps (Michael Fassbender), who, each in his own way, exploit his services.

In addition to being an exemplary cinematic experience, where language and aesthetics are perfectly developed, the feature goes down in history by portraying crudely, but without losing sensitivity, one of the most infamous periods of all time.
